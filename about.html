Platform Overview

Smart Lender

AI-Powered Loan Approval Prediction System

A banking-grade software solution using machine learning to predict the creditworthiness of loan applicants. The platform runs classification models on historical records to assist credit officers and analysts with rapid, data-informed approvals.

Our Mission

To eliminate the friction of loan application evaluation, reduce credit assessment times from days to milliseconds, and build risk mitigation systems that empower banking networks to lower default rates and secure loan assets.

Platform Objectives

  • Automate credit qualification using mathematical classifiers.
  • Ensure data integrity through feature-level constraints.
  • Maximize predictability using state-of-the-art XGBoost algorithms.
  • Build cloud-scalable banking tools suitable for enterprise deployment.

Application & Assessment Guide

1

Required Loan Details

To run the predictive assessment, the following applicant parameters must be supplied:

  • Demographics: Gender, Marital Status, and number of Dependents.
  • Socio-Economics: Education level and self-employment status.
  • Financials: Applicant & co-applicant income (in ₹), plus existing monthly debts (EMIs).
  • Loan Specs: Requested loan amount (in thousands ₹), term duration, and property area type.
2

How to Process the Loan

Follow these operational steps to execute a credit assessment on the platform:

  1. Navigate to the Predict Loan portal in the navigation bar.
  2. Fill out all mandatory personal and financial inputs with verified data.
  3. Input the applicant's numeric Credit Score (300-850) and current active EMIs.
  4. Review the Credit Assessment Guidelines terms and check the consent box.
  5. Click Predict Loan to transmit features to the classification engine.
3

Checking Approval Status

The decision engine generates an interactive risk report comprising three distinct parameters:

  • Decision Ratio: Shows the ML model's confidence probability of approval/rejection.
  • Credit Score: Evaluates if the score satisfies the 750 underwriting score guidelines.
  • Debt Ratio (DTI): Checks if combined monthly debts stay within the 40% safety threshold.
  • Qualitative Factors: Lists primary approval strengths or red rejection factors detailing the decision trigger.
